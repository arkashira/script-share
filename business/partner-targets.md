## Partner Integration Roadmap for Script-Share

### Target Integrations

| Partner/API          | Free Tier Limits                      | Integration Effort | Value-Add (User Job Solved)                          | Revenue-Share Potential |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------|
| **GitHub**           | Unlimited public repositories         | M                  | Facilitates version control and collaboration on scripts. | Yes                     |
| **Google Drive**     | 15 GB storage                         | S                  | Easy sharing and storage of scripts and outputs for users. | Yes                     |
| **Zapier**           | 100 tasks/month                       | M                  | Automates workflows by connecting Script-Share with other apps (e.g., notifications, data input). | Yes                     |

| **Stripe**           | 0% transaction fees for the first $1M | M                  | Facilitates payment processing for premium features or services within Script-Share. | Yes                     |

### Rationale for Integration Choices

1. **GitHub**: As a widely used platform for code collaboration, integrating with GitHub allows users to manage their scripts effectively, track changes, and collaborate with others, which is essential for non-technical users who may need guidance.

2. **Google Drive**: Many users are familiar with Google Drive for file storage and sharing. This integration simplifies the process of sharing scripts and outputs, making it more accessible for non-technical users.

3. **Zapier**: This integration allows users to automate repetitive tasks, connecting Script-Share with other tools they already use, thereby enhancing productivity and reducing manual work.

4. **Slack**: By integrating with Slack, users can receive real-time updates and collaborate with team members, making it easier to discuss and troubleshoot scripts without leaving the platform.

5. **Jupyter Notebook**: While not a traditional SaaS, integrating with Jupyter allows users to leverage a powerful tool for running Python scripts in an interactive environment, catering to users who may be transitioning from coding to a more user-friendly interface.

6. **AWS Lambda**: This integration enables users to run scripts in a serverless environment, providing scalability and performance without the need for infrastructure management, appealing to users who may not have technical expertise.

7. **Twilio**: Adding SMS notifications allows users to receive immediate feedback on script execution, enhancing user engagement and ensuring they are informed of important updates.

8. **Stripe**: Integrating payment processing can facilitate monetization strategies for premium features, allowing users to access additional functionalities while providing a revenue stream for Script-Share. 

### Conclusion

The proposed integrations not only enhance the functionality of Script-Share but also align with the needs of mechanical engineers and scientists who require a collaborative and user-friendly platform for Python development. Prioritizing partners with revenue-share potential ensures sustainable growth and profitability for the product.
